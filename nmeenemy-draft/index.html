Understanding Herd Immunity

There are a couple common misconceptions about how immunizations work.

  1. Being vaccinated means you are now fully immune to the disease. FALSE

  2. If anti-vaxxers choose to not vaccinate then only THEY are placed at risk while the rest of us remain safe from infection. FALSE

What is Herd Immunity?

The term herd immunity has several meanings. According to the :

A common implication of the term is that the risk of infection among susceptible individuals in a population is reduced by the presence and proximity of immune individuals (this is sometimes referred to as “indirect protection” or a “herd effect”)

This means that your immunity to deadly diseases largely depends on the immunity of everyone around you. If others choose not to vaccinate, it puts you and your family's safety at risk.
